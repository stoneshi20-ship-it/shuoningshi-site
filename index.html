Product Design · Award-winning

AEGIS

The first ergonomic stationary bench designed specifically for shoulder training.

Free-weight training offers comprehensive benefits but carries real risk — especially for the shoulder. AEGIS sets the seat and backrest at 15° and 105° for optimal posture, adding lumbar and headrest support that follows the natural curve of the spine. Three adjustable mechanisms — headrest, lumbar cushion, and seat position — can all be tuned while seated, so users of any body type can find a safe, comfortable form in real time.

UI/UX · Augmented Reality

AquilaInsight

An AR interface that makes breast-cancer surgery more precise and safe.

Built on research from the Biosensor Lab at UIUC, AquilaInsight projects real-time imaging through AR glasses. Near-infrared cameras display tumor position and boundaries for precise resection, then locate sentinel lymph nodes via fluorescent staining. A dedicated simulation mode lets medical students rehearse the same procedures with the imaging used in real operations — designed around the distinct needs of surgeons and students.

Product Design · Smart Device

Naginata

An intelligent home grooming device that makes haircuts effortless at home.

Cutting hair at home saves time and money, but consistency is hard. Naginata pairs a mobile app that 3D-scans the head and previews hairstyles with a modular handheld device — interchangeable sideburn, nose-hair, beard, and smart-clipper modules. A depth camera and infrared fill light guide precise blade positioning, while a parametric rubber grip supports a secondary back-of-head hold for hard-to-reach areas.

UI/UX · Web Redesign

Motorola Razr+

A website redesign case study for the Razr+ × Pantone edition.

A one-week redesign sprint reimagining the Razr+ product page. The new flow streamlines a cluttered, repetitive spec layout — folding steps and showing only selectable items to keep attention on the purchase. A dynamic Pantone gallery uses video, stills, GIFs, and interactive visuals to create an immersive showcase, while scroll-, click-, and hover-based interactions add playful depth to browsing features.
